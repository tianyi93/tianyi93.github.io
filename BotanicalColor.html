Overview

Background

This was a course project where we worked with an actual client, Botanical Color. We re-designed pages on the Botanical Colors website to better cater to new user experiences and enhance the brand image in the natural dye industry.

Challenge

The challenge of this project was to understand the specialized content of the industry and re-organized the site information architecture. Also, find a balance between the client's and the user's needs.

About Botanical Color

Botanical Color is a company that sells natural dyes to artisans and industrial clients seeking the beauty of a sustainable natural color palette. The founder Kathy is a recognized authority on natural dyes, pigments and commercial applications using natural dyes. She has worked in the field since 2003. Services include retail, wholesale, customization, and various events related to natural dye.

Client’s vision

By visiting the studio and talking to the brand's founders, we learned that they not only want to improve the user experience but also want Botanical Color to be a platform for selling dyes, as well as a place for people to learn and understand natural dye. They wanted the site to be more of an advocate and educator.

Competitive Analysis

The analysis is based on three categories: Natural Products, Synthetic Dyes, and Advocacy. On each of the sites, their features and design are compared under five criteria. Combing the results with the Heuristics evaluation to the existing website, the key findings and recommendations are as follow:

Key findings

Recommendations

7/9 competitor websites have clear product descriptions (including pictures or videos) on the Homepage.

Add a product section to the home page.

8/9 competitor websites have mission statement section on the Homepage or About page.

Add a mission statement section.

3/9 competitor websites use pictures to visualize the drop down menu.

Consider adding images to the drop down menu.

User Research

We interviewed nine new users of the site, recorded their experiences, and collected user pain points. Then prioritize these pain points to determine the most critical issues for users. 

- Top issues: Confusing Banner/ Can't find brand & founder introduction / Confusing categories
- Secondary issues: Hard to sign up for an event / Excessive Content ( Too many words and the drop down too long) / Can’t find search bar

Style guide

Font

I hope these font can be great at setting a artsy mood and tone to the content giving it a more crafty look while being elegant. After search with team members, I decided to go with a minimal and decorative font such as Italiana and Cardo.

​

Colors

After visiting the workshop we asked Kathy which natural dye color is the most popular one and the answer was Indigo, therefore we decided to stick to the most popular dye color and used a range of secondary colors from the logo.

​

Buttons / Links

A large change to the current website is adding more buttons to the content. From our user testing 7/9 participants mentioned they did not know where to click to get to things when text are hyperlinked. So we added more buttons to our design to resolve this pain point.

Takeaways

This project has a unique feature: it is a particular industry, "natural dye." To build a better IA for the website, we spent a long time understanding its original content, which involved a lot of technical terms. This made me realize that design is not only about visual content but also about understanding content and logic.

​

During this collaboration, the client wanted to demonstrate their professionalism. However, through research, we found that new users could not understand the content and had difficulty finding what they wanted through the navigation bar. So we balanced the client's needs with the user's needs. Our solution is to replace some words and images were added to help users understand. This experience will allow me to balance the needs of different aspects in my future work.
